#1: Getting on the Brooklyn and Manhattan Bridges Sucks

(40.69603, -73.98453) in DUMBO is the site of more accidents than any other location in New York City—a whopping 585 of them—more than a hundred accidents a year.

The Google Street Maps view reveals what makes this location so accident-prone. This intersection is the first major street intersecting the off-ramp of the Brooklyn-Queens Expressway, a major arterial road for which this is the last exit before the Manhattan and Brooklyn bridges. Drivers commuting to or from Manhattan get off here and merge with locally inbound drivers also getting onto both bridges.

Considering that these two bridges together carry a huge chunk of the daily traffic into and out of Manhattan, all of which must merge on this one street, it seems obvious that such a problem would occur. Accidents at the foot of the bridges are so common that they likely have a significant negative impact on access to the bridges, and one wonders how tired the local NYPD precinct—located quite literally down the block—gets of getting called to this location. This is a horrible, mostly un-fixable intersection: of course the builders of the bridges were not aware of modern traffic concerns when they were completed in 1883 and 1909, respectively.

#2: The Queensboro is Hardly Better

Proving that bridge designers are equal-opportunity muck-ups, the second most accident-prone location in the city (and the most accident-prone one in Manhattan), with 472 accidents total (still over 100 a year), is the Manhattan on-ramp of the Queensboro Bridge.

The NYPD does seem to be doing its best keeping things moving, though. If there's any location that needs a 24/7 traffic officer it's this one.
